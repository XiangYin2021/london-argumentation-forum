Overview

A focused forum for argumentative AI.

Recent work in explainable and trustworthy AI has renewed interest in symbolic and hybrid methods for reasoning, dialogue and decision-making. Computational argumentation is a particularly promising paradigm for making reasons visible, contestable and useful to people.

The London Argumentation Forum brings together researchers working on formal foundations, applications and human-facing uses of argumentation. The workshop is designed for discussion: short presentations, generous questions and room for early-stage ideas.

Topics

Topics include, but are not limited to

01

Theoretical Foundations of Computational Argumentation

Formal models, semantics, non-monotonic reasoning, probabilistic argumentation, game-theoretic argumentation, and computational complexity.

02

Argumentation in Dialogue, Agents and Strategic Reasoning

Dialogue, negotiation, strategies, multi-agent systems, coordination, coalition formation, trust and reputation.

03

Argumentation for Explainable and Trustworthy AI

Argumentation-based explanation, argumentation-based decision making, human-computer interaction, and trustworthy AI.

04

Argumentation, Natural Language and Large Language Models

Argument mining, computational linguistics, argumentation and narrative, large language models, and neuro-symbolic computing with argumentation.

05

Argumentation Systems, Tools and Applications

Implementations, software tools, domain-specific systems, and innovative applications.

06

This Year's Special Themes

Argumentation for safety, and argumentation in education.

Submission

Submit a 500-word abstract.

Please email an abstract of no more than 500 words describing the direction you would like to present. Contributions may be based on already published work, ongoing work or unpublished ideas.

The forum does not currently have a programme committee or formal reviewing process. The aim is to create a high-quality discussion space for researchers interested in argumentation and related areas.
